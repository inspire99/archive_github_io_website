Azure Synapse Analytics - new era of Analytics and unification ?

Ganesh Swamypillai December 20, 2020

 





First of all, I am not so well versed with Analytics platforms - I mean - hands-dirty expertise - well-versedness. But, I can definitely appreciate the capabilities, when a platform showcases its capabilities, for example: Apache Spark is able to derive insights out of some non-relational data.

At the end of the day, you have some data collected from the past, for example, region-wise or age-wise shopping behavior of users. And you want to derive insights out of this data, probably, you might come into conclusion, by writing queries. As we have data, all we can do is query and find the results.

For example: Which item was most sold in this month? Which item was most viewed on any particular day? etc etc..

For these, developers or data analysts can write queries or logical stuff, to back-track from there and derive relationships, so that, they are able to pull unrelated variables together and impact business with this new set of insights. For example, there could be some NetFlix shows focusing on Chess game, suddenly there's a spike of interest in Chess game, people watching chess games on youtube. People might start buying chess books or people might start signing up for Chess coaches.

So, is NetFlix a related variable to Flipkart in any sense? Why should someone care about it ? Probably not. But, in this age of data, everything is related. In this age of internet, everything is related to each other. What a person goes through their lives, affects their decisions. And nowadays, most of the time, on an average, people spend large amount of their personal time on YouTube or other social media. And all this data is being collected and 'served' and interested parties are able to make decisions. 

So, backtracking from a person's life, people can write queries with the data. And derive insights.

The same could be done with any other domains, for example: medicine or climate or education. 

Having deviated from the main topic - Azure Synapse Analytics - let me come back to it.

As I mentioned, having seen the capabilities of Azure Synapse Analytics - it is basically a unification platform or , in other words, its an integration platform or end to end platform of entire analytics lifecycle. 

It is able to bring together - sourcing data, enriching data, querying data, learning from data, producing data - all this under one roof.

It is also able to bring together - collaborators - under one roof.

Collaborators - Data engineers, Data analysts, Machine learning specialists, data warehouse specialists - under one roof - under one service.

This leads to my conclusion that, it is a new era of Analytics , probably this will also inspire other cloud providers, as to their cloud offerings. 

Probably, this also leads to differentiation thinking - as to how to differentiate your cloud offerings from other cloud providers.

Try to understand or learn from customer difficulties and bring some unification platform - which might cater to special set of customers, but, then , it might capture the market.

For example, in this case, as I read, Walgreens, Myntra were benefitted from this.

Others will follow.

For differentiation - focus on Unification ?
